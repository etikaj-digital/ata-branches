ATA Adelaide branch meeting - Sundrop Farms – sustainable food production

  • 2017-05-15 09:30:00
  • 32 Rosa St, Goodwood SA

When: Monday 15 May 2017,  7.30pm sharp

Where: Goodwood Community Centre (Banquet Hall), 32 Rosa St, Goodwood SA. The centre is convenient to the tram, buses and train. Enter the car park via Florence St.

Cost: ATA members $2. Non-members $5, tea, coffee and biscuits provided.

Presentation:  Frazer-Nash Consultancy (FNC) has been closely involved in the design, construction and performance monitoring of the water, energy and growing systems at Sundrop Farms’ extensive operation at Port Augusta.

Marc Lyell, FNC Business Manager, will explain the seawater purification, solar thermal electricity generation, food production and energy efficiency features of this highly innovative facility. He’ll overview their design philosophy, performance and future directions.

We’ve been keen to host a presentation about Sundrop Farms for some time so please publicise this to anyone who you think will be interested.

Adelaide branch committee election: As announced in the March newsletter an election for the positions of Convenor, Treasurer and Committee will be held.

Seeley visit

The visit to Seeley International was well attended and members enjoyed an extensive two hour tour of the manufacturing operations.

Other events

The Adelaide Sustainable Building Network will host two forums on May 1st and 2nd: Creating a Positive Impact and Building Nutrition. See the ASBN Facebook page here for details.

Q&A

ATA meetings put people with questions in touch with people who might have answers. The Q&A session is an open discussion about sustainability topics.

As always, the Committee invites suggestions for presentations and feedback regarding what they want from ATA meetings - send to adelaidebranch@ata.org.au

Meetings are open to ATA members and non-members. They aim to be informative and interactive in helping people to learn about living and building sustainability. We look forward to seeing you.
