Adelaide Branch Meeting - 17 Feb 2014 - Green infrastructure: Green walls, green roofs and water sensitive urban design

  • 2014-02-17 19:30:00
  • 34 Rosa St, Goodwood, SA

Time: 7.30pm sharp

Where: Goodwood Community Centre (Banquet Hall), 32/34 Rosa St, Goodwood SA (Goodwood Community Centre is convenient to the tram (stop 3), buses and train. Enter the car park via Florence St.). Click here for a map of the venue.

Entry fee: ATA members $2. Non-members $5.

Tea, coffee and biscuits provided.

Presenter: Simon Beecham is Head of the School of Natural and Built Environments at the University of SA and was previously the Director of their Centre for Water Management and Reuse. He will examine the role of green walls and roofs in water sensitive urban design and give an overview of recent developments in water collection and management techniques, in particular those developed by UniSA. for example:

  • Permeable pavement treatment and reuse systems
  • Siphonic harvesting systems for commercial and industrial buildings
  • Vegetated stormwater and greywater treatment systems
  • Green infrastructure and heat island effects
  • Stormwater treatment and management
  • Reclaimed water in agriculture and aquaculture.

Q&A session

ATA meetings put people with questions in touch with people who might have answers. The Q&A session after the main presentation is an open discussion about sustainability topics. For example:

  • Who can help with an issue?
  • What\'s on in other parts of Adelaide, SA and Australia?
  • Any events you\'ve been to and what you\'ve learned.
  • What presentations or field trips would you\'d like?
