ATA Adelaide Branch Meeting - Marine algae to biofuels

  • 2014-10-20 08:30:00
  • 34 Rosa St, Goodwood SA

When: Monday 20th October 2014, 7.30pm sharp

Where: Goodwood Community Centre (Banquet Hall), 32/34 Rosa St, Goodwood SA Goodwood Community Centre is convenient to the tram, buses and train. Enter the car park via Florence St.

Entry fee: ATA members $2. Non-members $5, Tea, coffee and biscuits provided.

Presenter: Ollie Clark AM, Chairman of Muradel Pty Ltd.
Ollie has a long and illustrious career in Australia’s energy sector, including as inaugural Managing Director of Envestra Pty Ltd. Over the last decade, Ollie has chaired two companies, SQC Pty Ltd and Muradel Pty Ltd, working on the production of “green” crude oil from microalgae.

Muradel originated from a joint research project between Murdoch University, Adelaide University and SQC Pty Ltd. This aimed to demonstrate the feasibility of producing biofuel feedstock from marine microalgae.

Research included determining how the ingredients needed for growing algae - carbon dioxide, nitrogen, phosphorous and water – could be recycled from as many sources as possible. It would also ensure the green crude derived from algae would be suitable for processing in existing oil refineries into traditional liquid fuels.

The process was developed at a pilot plant at Karratha WA. A demonstration plant has been commissioned at Whyalla SA.

Production uses sustainable and energy efficient technologies:

  • Energy efficiency is built into every step of the process, from the pond design to the pump selection, to extraction techniques.
  • Algae are grown in open raceway ponds using seawater. This avoids using costly freshwater and allows natural management of contaminating microorganisms.
  • Low energy continuous flocculation and concentration processes create a concentrated product that is suitable for direct treatment into biofuels.
  • Nutrients are recycled to minimise waste as well as reducing cost.
  • Power consumption is continuously monitored and calculated to ensure a net positive energy balance.

Come along to hear about the latest developments in this fascinating technology.

Q&A session
