ATA Adelaide branch meeting: Monica Oliphant AO, Renewable Energy in China

  • 2015-06-15 09:30:00
  • 34 Rosa St, Goodwood SA

When: Monday 15th June 2015
Time: 7.30pm sharp
Where: Goodwood Community Centre (Banquet Hall), 32/34 Rosa St, Goodwood SA Goodwood Community Centre is convenient to the tram, buses and train. Enter the car park via Florence St.
Cost: ATA members $2. Non-members $5, Tea, coffee and biscuits provided.

Presentation: Monica Oliphant is an Adjunct Associate Professor at UniSA and Past President of the International Solar Energy Society. Formerly an Energy Research Scientist with ETSA she\'s now an independent consultant specialising in energy efficiency and renewable energy. Monica travels frequently on speaking and study engagements and was recently appointed as Team Leader of a UN University study into establishing an Institute for Renewable Energy in China. Last Monday 8 June 2015, she was appointed as an Officer of the Order of Australia in the Queen\'s Birthday Honours List.

Monica will present an overview of renewable energy in China, especially projects in Shandong Province and China\'s Solar Valley.

Wed 24 June, 6:30 pm, Adelaide Sustainable Building Network, 87 Gibson St, Bowden: Nick and Ross Harding will discuss how we how we can overcome cultural barriers to self sufficiency in Australian cities. See their Facebook pages here or email: info@adelaidesbn.com.au.

Wind monitoring instruments

The branch has a number of anemometers (Windsensor P2546A) and wind vanes (Vector Instruments W200P) that are available to members for a donation of $5 each. They are professional quality instruments but in used condition and for accurate measurement would require calibration. See the web for details. They don\'t have interface electronics and anemometers require a plug and cable. They will be available for inspection at our June meeting.

Q&A

ATA meetings put people with questions in touch with people who might have answers. The Q&A session is an open discussion about sustainability topics.

Meetings are open to ATA members and non-members. They aim to be informative and interactive in helping people to learn about living and building sustainability. We look forward to seeing you.
