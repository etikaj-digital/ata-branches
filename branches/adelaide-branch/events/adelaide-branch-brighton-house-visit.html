Adelaide Branch - Brighton House Visit

  • 2014-08-11 08:30:00

When: Monday 11 August 2014, 6.30pm to 8.00pm

Where: Location given on RSVP. ATA members only – (Maximum 25 visitors)

A winters evening visit to a two-storied house of straw bale – come and feel the difference!

Finn and Chantel will show you their special home, talk about their building journey and how the house has been to live in over the last year.

The house is made mainly of Straw bales and wood with recycled bricks thrown in for good measure.

Designed by Bohdan Dorniak, the design brief was for a comfortable, fun and sustainable home for a family of five.

Come and see what you think of the finished house.

Features:

  • Strawbale walls (obviously)
  • Recycled red bricks.
  • Radial sawn cladding on the outside.
  • Solar PV & Hot Water.
  • Part recycled Jarrah kitchen.
  • Posts from South Melbourne docks.
  • Flying ducks.
  • Native garden with veggie patch.
  • 60-year-old vines off a pergola.
  • Water tanks.
  • Louvre windows to get the cross breeze & create ventilation.
  • No Air conditioning
  • A straw bale courtyard with Hammock and house number made from bike bits.
  • Ceiling in bedrooms are colorbond corrugated iron roofing material.
  • Garage for bikes, not cars.

And more…
