ATA Adelaide branch meeting - Rebuilding bushfire-devastated homes and lives

  • 2016-04-18 09:30:00
  • 34 Rosa St, Goodwood SA

When: Monday 18 April 2016, 7.30pm sharp.

Where: Goodwood Community Centre (Banquet Hall), 32/34 Rosa St, Goodwood SA Goodwood Community Centre is convenient to the tram, buses and train. Enter the car park via Florence St.

Cost: ATA members $2. Non-members $5, tea, coffee and biscuits provided.

Presentation

Architect Emilis Prelgauskas is a regular and popular presenter at ATA meetings who usually talks about energy-efficient building design. Tonight he’ll describe his community involvement following devastating bushfires such as Sampson Flat and Pinery. He and other professionals provided pro bono advice to bushfire-affected families about rebuilding their homes and their lives.

Emilis observes the link between where we live and the people we are. Conserving the best of the past, preparing for the ‘new normal’, meeting current regulations and dealing with insurance claims is a challenge. His decades of experience with building processes and bushfire response allow him to make a positive contribution to this complex mix.

Emilis has been involved in 14 fire events in the last 5 years including logistics for veterinarians and triaging animals. He’s the author of the ‘Bushfire’ section of the Federal Government’s ‘Your Home Technical Manual\', Edition 4.
