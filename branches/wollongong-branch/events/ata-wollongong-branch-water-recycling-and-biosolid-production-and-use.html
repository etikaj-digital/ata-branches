ATA Wollongong branch - Water Recycling and Biosolid Production and Use

  • 2017-11-02 08:00:00
  • Innovation Way, North Wollongong NSW 2500

When: Thursday 2 November 2017, 6:30pm for 7:00pm start

Where: UOW Innovation Campus (Squires Way, North Wollongong) - ITAMS Building, building 233, room G01.

Click here for a Google Map and here for a campus map. Parking is free from 6:30 in P1 (near Science Centre) or P5 (south of Building 233)

Cost: A gold coin donation to cover refreshments would be appreciated.

Presentation: Hugo Figgis - Production Officer, Wollongong Water Recycling Plant
Water recycling: Wollongong recycles about 40% of its wastewater on an average day, which is one of the highest recycling rates of any Australian city. Hugo will give the details and highlight the residues (biosolids) extracted and their benefits to farmers and others.
Biosolids: The solid material removed from wastewater is nutrient-rich, and can be beneficially applied to farmland or composted to further improve its use in fertilising and soil conditioning. The digestion process also provides benefits from renewable energy generation.
