ATA Melbourne Branch meeting - Living in a Passive Solar Strawbale house: successes and lessons learnt

  • 2014-05-21 19:00:00
  • 397 Burwood Rd, Hawthorn VIC 3122

Where: Swinburne University of Technology, Hawthorn Campus (Engineering Building – Room EN213). Click here for directions.

Entry: Gold Coin Donation to cover catering costs.

Colin Goodwin speaks about the novel self sufficient passive solar post and beam strawbale house that he and partner Libby Anthony designed and built in cool wet foothills of the Strezelecki Ranges in South Gippsland. Their focus was on simplicity, functionality, beauty, comfort and sustainability. They have also logged it’s performance over a full year, and Colin will discuss the building of the house and how it’s meeting expectations.

Colin Goodwin is a Telecoms Engineer and Marketer. His partner Libby Anthony is an Environmental Scientist who has worked at State and Local levels in Victoria. Both are long term members of the ATA; Libby was the ATA’s CEO for many years.

Convenor: Chris Moss

Followed by discussion and refreshments

Note: Slides and video from previous meetings are available at the ATA Melbourne Branch website.
