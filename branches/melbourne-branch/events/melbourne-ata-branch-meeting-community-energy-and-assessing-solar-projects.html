Melbourne ATA Branch Meeting - Community Energy and assessing Solar Projects

  • 2014-07-16 09:00:00
  • 397 Burwood Rd, Hawthorn VIC 3122

Where: Swinburne University of Technology, Hawthorn Campus (Engineering Building – Room EN213). Click here for directions.

Entry: Gold Coin Donation to cover costs.

Update and findings from recent groundbreaking ATA economic research projects:

Presenter: Craig Memery, the ATA\'s energy consumer advocate, will report back on the Community Energy Conference, the  development of the national Community Energy Strategy, and how we can get involved.  He will explain some of the ATAs economic research projects including a comparison of gas vs. electricity, and changes in the economics of water saving technologies. Craig will also give an overview of the ATAs new Sunulator tool for verifying the viability of PV systems used in a community system environment..

Craig is a consumer advocate and specialist in energy technology, networks and markets, and has worked in the sustainable energy sector since 2002. With ATA, his role is divided between providing expert advice to clients in the government, private and community sectors, and advocating on behalf of all Australian energy consumers for improvements to Australia’s energy markets and systems.

Peter Wonfor, who also attended the Conference will provide a brief overview of the conference as an introduction to Craig’s presentation.

Note: Slides and video from previous meetings are available at the ATA Melbourne Branch website. The video from this meeting can be found on our Youtube channel here.

Please pass this invitation on to others! Visitors are very welcome.

Convenor: Chris Moss

Followed by discussion and refreshments
