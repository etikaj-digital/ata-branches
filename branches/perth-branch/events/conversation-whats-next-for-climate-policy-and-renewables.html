Conversation: What’s next for climate policy and renewables?

  • 2014-04-28 17:30:00
  • 140 Royal St, East Perth

You are invited to attend: “Conversation: What’s next for climate policy and renewables?”

When: Monday, April 28, 2014 from 5:30 pm - 7:30 pm (Free event. Nibbles and Networking from 5:30pm for a 6:00pm start)
Where: Lecture Theatre, 2nd Level Building B, Central TAFE, East Perth Campus, 140 Royal St, East Perth

At this SEN event, hear from experts in the field of renewable energy and climate change policy as they analyse the challenges and opportunities arising from the results of the recent Senate by-election. How can we best influence federal policy and what is your role?

Industry Representatives:
Wind: Andrew Woodroffe co-founded “SkyFarming”, which was involved in both Mt Barker and Denmark Windfarms from conception to commissioning.
Solar: Travis Hargreaves is an active member of the Alternative Technology Association and Australian Solar Council and runs a business “Sunday Energy”.
Wave: Tim Sawyer is Project Development Office, Marine Renewables at Carnegie Wave Energy, which will produce both clean energy and fresh water.
Energy: Kirsten Rose is CEO of Sustainable Energy Association, the peak business body for the sustainable energy industry.

Advocacy Group Representatives:
Piers Verstegen, Conservation Council of WA
Tanyia Maxted, 350 Perth
Tess Stapleton-Clark, Australian Youth Climate Coalition WA
