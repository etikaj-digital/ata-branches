ATA Forum: Rail Transport in Tasmania

  • 2014-02-20 19:30:00
  • 32 East Barrack Street, Deloraine TAS, 7304
When: Thursday 20 February 2014
Time: 7:30pm
Where: Deloraine Primary School Hall. 32 East Barrack Street, Deloraine TAS, 7304. Click here for a map.
Cost: Free.

How would you like to step on a train at a local railway station and be transported at a leisurely rate to the nearest big city?

How could we organise such a system in Tasmania? Is it economically realistic? What factors have to be taken into account? Why should we do it?

For answers to these and other questions, come along to the next ATA forum on responding to climate change.

The program includes;

  • Helen Hutchinson and Doug Rolfe from the ATA on the relevance considering rail as a transport option in response to Peak Oil and Climate Change
  • A brief history of rail in Tasmania from Bill Housego
  • Local MP, Tim Morris, Greens spokesperson for transport on rail in Tasmania today
  • Peter Mackenzie on the justification of Rail vs Road
  • Toby Rowallan from Future Transport Tasmania on local rail networks - the Hobart experience
  • Questions for the panel
  • Where to now?

There will be plenty of opportunity to talk after the meeting over tea and biscuits.

If you know anyone else who may be interested please bring them along.
