ATA Melbourne EV branch meeting - Current battery technology explained and the future 1000km Metal-Air batteries

  • 2014-04-30 19:00:00
  • 397 Burwood road, Hawthorn VIC 3122

When: Wednesday, April 30 2014 (please note the change of date)
Time: 7:00pm
Where: The meeting will be held in the Engineering Building (EN) of Swinburne University, Hawthorn campus. Optional dinner from 6pm at Funky Curry.Â For a map of Swinburne click here.

Presenter: Paul Paton is an electric vehicle owner and enthusiast. He was a franchise electronic retailer of computers, mobile phones and consumer electronics. He studied first business management and then nursing at Swinburne University and currently builds and repairs battery systems for electric vehicles. In 2010 he designed and built a Li-Ion / NiMH hybrid battery and installed long range vehicle batteries achieving a range of over 300km.
Paul\'s presentation will cover current Lithium battery types. Comparing their performance, advantages and disadvantages. He will also cover the battery management systems used and reasons for early failure, which battery systems are used by the leading EV manufactures such as Tesla and Nissan including why they chose them. Lastly improvements underway in Li-Ion battery manufacturing and research on future battery technologies with 4 times the energy density of current Li-Ion batteries.
