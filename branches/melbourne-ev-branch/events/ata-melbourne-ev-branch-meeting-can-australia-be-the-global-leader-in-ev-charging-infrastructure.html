ATA Melbourne EV branch meeting - Can Australia be the global leader in EV Charging Infrastructure?

  • 2015-08-26 09:00:00
  • 397 Burwood road, Hawthorn VIC 3122

When: Wednesday 26 August 2015, 7:00pm
Where: Swinburne University, Hawthorn campus, Burwood road, Hawthorn, Room EN615. If you have trouble locating the Engineering Building (EN) then you look at this Hawthorn campus map.

For the past few years, Tim Washington, founder of JET Charge, has been asking the same question over and over again. The question is so superficially simple, but so complex in reality.

Can Australia, a country that has lagged behind the developed world in the adoption of EVs, actually become a global leader in EV Charging Infrastructure?

In this session Tim will ask you to join him in a conversation around the future of EV Charging infrastructure in Australia.

Notably, he wants to explore:

  • How do we set the right AC and DC charging standard?
  • Should we support open or closed networks?
  • Where does wireless charging fit in?
  • Is there a role for utilities to play?
  • How can stationary battery storage help EV Charging infrastructure?

Tim will also share his experiences lobbying for more EV Charging infrastructure in Australia, and the work JET Charge has done, and will continue to do, to pursue this end.

Tim will also bring along some domestic EVSE products that form part of the JET Charge range.
