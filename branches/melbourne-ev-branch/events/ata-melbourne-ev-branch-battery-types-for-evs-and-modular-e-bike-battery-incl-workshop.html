ATA Melbourne EV branch - Battery Types for EVs and Modular E-Bike Battery (incl. Workshop)

  • 2015-03-25 08:00:00
  • 397 Burwood road, Hawthorn VIC 3122

When: Wednesday 25 March 2015, 7:00pm

Where: Swinburne University, Hawthorn campus, Burwood road, Hawthorn, Room EN102. If you have trouble locating the Engineering Building (EN) then you look at this Hawthorn campus map.

This month there will be an updated presentation on batteries types available for Electric Vehicles. Telsa has announced a 400 mile battery and aerodynamics package for the Tesla Roadster. CALB has released the CAM72 series for home built EV’s with extended range. The presentation will also have tables on different Li-Ion chemistries and what types of vehicles they suit.

Modular system for assembling e-bike batteries: Paul Paton has developed for our members a modular system for building low cost battery packs. This system suits 12 volt Li-Ion battery replacements, 24-48 volt e-bikes or 60 volt high performance bikes.

A workshop will be held on the night where you will be able to build any battery from the modules supplied. This includes mounting plates to fit to a bike. A 36 volt 10 amp/hr battery would cost $110. Assembly would take 30 minutes. Tools for assembly including tab welder and hot melt glue gun will be supplied.
