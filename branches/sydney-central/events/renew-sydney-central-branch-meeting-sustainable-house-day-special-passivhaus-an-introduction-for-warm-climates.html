Renew Sydney Central Branch meeting - Sustainable House Day Special - Passivhaus - An Introduction for Warm Climates

  • 2018-09-10 08:30:00
  • 4-12 Buckland St, Chippendale NSW 2008

When: Monday 10 September 2018, 6:00pm for a 6:30pm start.
Where: EnergyLab, 4-12 Buckland St, Chippendale NSW 2008 (click here and here for maps).
Cost: Due to the change of venue and associated costs, a $5 donation would be appreciated.

Note: that the talk starts at 6.30pm and finishes at 7.30pm with time before and after to chat with other attendees and the presenters.

Presentation:

Andy Marlow is an architect and Certified Passive House Designer. He is a director of Envirotecture and a volunteer Board member of the Australian Passive House Association. Andy is driven by a desire for all people to live, work and play in buildings that make them happier. He believes that good design should be accessible to and benefit all, not just those who commission it.

He has led master planning projects, been involved in several successful competitions including the Bathurst Sustainable Lifestyle House, and led the materials research for Australia’s first (almost) certified Living Building under the arduous Living Building Challenge – the Sustainable Buildings Research Centre at the University of Wollongong.

The evening will present the key fundamentals of Passivhaus including where and when it is an appropriate design strategy. The technical parameters will be explored and some of the possible design solutions will be presented and discussed.

A Passive House retrofit project (EnerPHit) will be used to demonstrate how the Passive House Planning Package can be cost-effectively utilised to optimise design solutions and highlight the biggest challenges in a Sydney climate.
