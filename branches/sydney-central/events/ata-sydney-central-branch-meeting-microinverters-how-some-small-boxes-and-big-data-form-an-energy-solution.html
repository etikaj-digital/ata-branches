ATA Sydney Central branch meeting - Microinverters: How some Small Boxes and Big Data form an Energy Solution

  • 2015-04-13 08:30:00
  • 101 Chalmers St, Surry Hills, NSW

When: Monday 13 April 2015, 6:00pm for 6:30pm - 7:30pm (Please note new meeting day)
Where: Interface head office and showroom, 101 Chalmers St Surry Hills (Please note new venue, click here for a map)

The rise of microinverters seems to follow the possibilities afforded by Moore\'s Law but what happens when small meets big? This is a great opportunity to learn more about the potential of microinverters as well as getting a sneak preview of the Enphase energy storage platform that will be released later this year.

Speaker: Keera Single studied Photovoltaic Engineering, Japanese, and International Business at both UNSW, and Tohoku University, Japan. Starting at Conergy in 2006, Keera oversaw PV product management, logistics, and engineering services as Technical Services Manager and held a seat on the Clean Energy Council PV Directorate. After 4 years with Conergy, Keera began consulting for Global Sustainable Energy Solutions in 2010, where he was responsible for training electricians in the CEC Accreditation course. Keera joined Q-Cells in late 2010 as Business Development Manager, bringing his technical knowledge and sales skills, and experience in working with Australian solar companies, before moving to Enphase in 2014 as Strategic Account Manager where he is the first point of contact for major account partners in NSW, ACT, and WA.

Talk summary:

  • Microinverters - what they are, and how they differ from traditional inverters
  • The importance of proactive monitoring, for consumers, installers, and utilities
  • AC batteries and why Australia is poised to take up storage
