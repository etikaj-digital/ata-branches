Sydney Central ATA Meeting - Putting energy back in the hands of communities

  • 2014-08-12 08:30:00
  • 320 Pitt St Sydney

When: Tuesday 12 August 2014, 6:00pm for 6:30pm
Where: Wallaby Room, 11th Floor, Telstra Building, 320 Pitt St Sydney

Presenter: Craig Memery is a consumer advocate and specialist in energy technology, networks and markets, and has worked in the sustainable energy sector since 2002. With ATA, his role is divided between providing expert advice to clients in the government, private and community sectors, and advocating on behalf of all Australian energy consumers for improvements to Australia’s energy markets and systems.

Craig joins us to discuss some of ATA’s recent ground breaking research, advocacy for Australia’s energy users, and involvement in the growing community energy sector. The talk will cover:
Community energy: a snapshot of the community energy sector, the ATA’s role in the Coalition for Community Energy and more information on how you can get involved.
Energy consumer advocacy: an overview of what’s wrong with Australia’s energy market, the ATA’s policy objectives, key challenges and recent wins.
Ground-breaking economic research: updates and findings from recent ATA economic research projects including:

  • Gas versus electricity – is it time to ditch the gas network?
  • Sunulator – Australia’s most advanced tool for analysing potential PV installations
  • Going offgrid – the outlook for homes and communities to have independent power supply
  • Water saving technology

Members and non-members are asked to register by selecting ATTEND at: atasydney.eventbrite.com.au

Please arrive at 6:00pm for a 6:30pm start. Wait in the Telstra Building foyer to be escorted to the lifts. Tea, coffee and tasty snacks are available before and after the talk, with gold coin donations gratefully accepted.

The ATA is an independent organisation that does not endorse products and seeks to provide members with information so that they can make well-informed sustainable decisions.
