Sydney Central ATA April Meeting: \&#039;Going Electric @ Google\&#039; &amp; \&#039;Vintage Electric: When Old Goes Better Than New\&#039;

  • 2014-04-08 18:00:00
  • 320 Pitt St Sydney NSW

Where: Wallaby Room, 11th Floor, Telstra Building, 320 Pitt St Sydney

Thanks to all who made it to the March event and for the robust discussion around the importance of more sustainably-thinking practitioners in the construction industry. In April we are looking at electric cars from two very different perspectives: from how they\'ve been integrated for use within a corporation to enable collaborative use and from a maverick leader operating outside of the norm who is going to be turning heads with his electric Citroen DS. Electric cars are coming and these two unique speakers will explain some of the many ways that they will arrive on our streets.

Summary: Going Electric @ Google by Sam Thorogood

Sam Thorogood is a senior software engineer at Google Australia, and in his "20% time" works on small projects, all with a sustainability angle. One of these projects is a car share scheme, called GoCred, that rewards Googlers for walking or otherwise commuting sustainably to the Sydney office with the use of two electric vehicles. These cars may be used for work errands, but also be taken home overnight or on weekends.
To learn more about the project: http://google-au.blogspot.com.au/2012/06/trip-report-green-cars.html

Summary: Vintage Electric when Old Goes Better than New by Aldo Grech
Aldo Grech works to help people discover their leadership qualities through his skills as a mentor and leader. He is particularly passionate about Citroens and the lines of the classic DS model are those of a sculpture in motion. Retrofitting a vintage car to become electric comes with challenges but the rewards are more than worth it. This is an opportunity to learn about the process and see what is being achieved today in a vintage electric car, when old becomes better than new. Check out the progress so far on this awesome project: http://amanoauto.blogspot.com.au/

Members and non-members are asked to register by selecting ATTEND at:  atasydney.eventbrite.com.au

Please arrive at 6:00pm for a 6:30pm start. Wait in the Telstra Building foyer or outside the door to be escorted to the lifts, then sign-in on arrival upstairs. Tea, coffee and tasty snacks are available before and after the talk, with gold coin donations gratefully accepted.
The ATA is an independent organisation that does not endorse products and seeks to provide members with information so that they can make well-informed sustainable decisions.
