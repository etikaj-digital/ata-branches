ATA Sydney Central meeting - Electric Vehicles - Getting off the Mark...

  • 2015-10-12 07:30:00
  • 101 Chalmers St, Surry Hills, NSW

When: Monday 12t October, 6:00pm to a 6:30-7:30pm start.

Where: Interface head office and showroom, 101 Chalmers St Surry Hills (click here for a map).

This month we are going electric as we look at what is happening with electric vehicles in Australia. We have a tag team of presenters that will fill you in on everything from charging infrastructure to the dizzying speed of the ELMOFO racing team.

It is hard to believe that it is already our last event at Interface this year!  We are busy planning for a collaborative event with GreenUps on community renewable energy at 107 Projects in Redfern for our final event of the year which promises to be a great night.

Speakers: Kymberly Noronha and Jaron Ware

Kymberly Noronha is from Recharging NSW, a company formed to meet the gap in electric vehicle infrastructure in Australia, Kymberly is the community partnerships and sales manager.  Kymberly and Gabriel share the driving of the families Electric Vehicle, a 2012 Mitsubishi I-MiEV.

Jaron Ware is the founder of Batrium Technologies that provides specialised battery management systems for the various lithium chemistries and formats to suit small to medium energy storage projects. Supplying BMS solutions to electric vehicles, off-grid solar, military robots and other bespoke designs. He has been part of the ELMOFO (Electric Radical SR8) racing team and part of their success in the NSW 2014 Super-sports championships.  Jaron has been involved in a numerous EV projects since 2009 and is the current AEVA representative at Standards Australia for the EV working group.

Talk summaries:

Kymberly will present on How you can help electric vehicles take off in Australia

  • Why electric vehicles are important,
  • What electric vehicles are available,
  • Barriers to electric vehicle uptake,
  • The effect on the grid and power requirements they have,
  • What is being done to encourage electric vehicle ownership and
  • What you can do to help.

Jaron is going to focus on Electric Vehicles and Lithium battery energy storage, what are the pit falls?

  • What is happening in Standards Australia (EV working group)
  • ELMOFO - electric racing against fuel vehicles in the NSW super sports category
  • When designing a lithium battery pack what needs to be considered?
  • Are people still converting cars to electric?
  • How have lithium batteries performed over the years and do they measure up?
  • What is second life in lithium batteries?

To get yourself revved up for the evening check out this clip of the ELMOFO Newcastle Hill Climb from last weekend.

Directions and further details:

Please arrive at 6:00pm for a 6:30pm start.

The Interface head office and showroom is located within the heritage-listed 101 Chalmers Street building in Sydney.  Access from Central Station involves turning right as you leave the Devonshire St exit, and then a short walk with a second, almost immediate right - if you get to to Prince Alfred Park you have gone too far!

Look for a two-storey red brick and sandstone building that adjoins a single-storey library, known as the ‘Railway Institute Building’. Entrance is at the rear of this building that has been faithfully restored and redesigned with stunning interiors, turning it into a showcase of environmental sustainability.  Due to our proximity to Central Station, we encourage attendees to catch public transport to this event as nearby parking is limited.

Tea, coffee and tasty snacks are available before and after the talk, with donations gratefully accepted.

The ATA is an independent organisation that does not endorse products and seeks to provide members with information so that they can make well-informed sustainable decisions.
