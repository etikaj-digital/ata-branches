Renew Sydney Central Branch meeting - Why water tanks matter

  • 2018-10-08 08:30:00
  • 4-12 Buckland St, Chippendale NSW 2008

When: Monday 8 October 2018, 6:00pm for a 6:30pm start.
Where: EnergyLab, 4-12 Buckland St, Chippendale NSW 2008 (click here and here for maps).
Cost: A $5 donation would be appreciated to assist with venue costs.

Presentation:

David Knights is an environmental engineer with more than 16 years experience in sustainable water management, waterway restoration and green infrastructure currently working for McGregor Coxall. He has been developing Water Sensitive Urban Design strategies and green infrastructure designs for various clients throughout Australia and internationally. He has been involved in all levels of Green Infrastructure Design from strategic planning and conceptual design to construction.

Talk summary: Our talk will look at the practical measures that you can take at household-scale with a particular focus on designing and installing rainwater tank systems.

  • Understanding rainwater tanks and their use and function in the landscape
  • Key things to consider including maintaining water quality and meeting health requirements
  • The three basic rules of rainwater tanks; size, roof area and what to connect to
  • Practical design considerations (pumps, plumbing, etc.)

And if you are in the process of designing a rainwater tank system don’t forget about the Renew’s Tankulator a fantastic online tool that will help you get the size of your rainwater tank right!
