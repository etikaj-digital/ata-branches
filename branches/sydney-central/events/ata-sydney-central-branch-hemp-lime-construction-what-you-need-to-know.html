ATA Sydney Central branch - Hemp lime construction - What you need to know

  • 2014-11-11 07:30:00
  • 320 Pitt St Sydney

When: Tuesday 11th November, 6:00pm for 6:30pm

Where: Wallaby Room, 11th Floor, Telstra Building, 320 Pitt St Sydney

Coming up is our last speaker event of the year with our Christmas get together on next month. This month our talk will be introduced by Dick Clarke the director of Envirotecture. Dick has edited the newly-released Rethink Building Materials, a valuable contribution to sustainable architecture. In his words the book "does not tell you what to think, but what to think about”.

Speaker: Klara Marosszeky has been involved in the NSW Hemp Industry in both hemp building and farming research since 1999. She is passionate about industrial hemp, for bioremediation, carbon management, and construction. Hemp provides unmatched benefits for consumers, the environment and the Australian economy. Klara provides training in hemp building design and installation and has worked as Project Manager for Ecological Sustainability Initiatives at the North Coast TAFE. She is a qualified home sustainability assessor and educator and is acutely aware of the need to manage greenhouse gas levels and the opportunities of building healthy, sustainable, and low embodied energy housing.

Talk summary: Research at UNSW between 2000 and 2006 produced an Australian Hemp Lime Binder and Render.  These materials have been used in eight new Australian residential builds and four retrofits and were a key design feature of the recently-built Green Gallery and Heritage Museum of Singapore Botanic Gardens. Excitingly 2014 will see the first real of expansion in hemp production in NSW increasing the potential for hemp construction significantly.

Hemp lime construction materials are very versatile. They have application in retrofitting existing buildings for energy efficiency and in new residential and commercial construction. A pioneering example was the construction of the Adam’s Brewery and Distribution Centre in Suffolk a 2400m2 warehouse that uses hemp lime blocks in walls to achieve a U-value of 0.18W/m2K.  

The talk will cover:

  • Hemp’s uses in varying forms as insulation in roofing, flooring and walls

  • Ensuring optimal performance in hemp lime construction

  • How hemp walls breathe, eliminating the growth of allergy causing moulds

  • Hemp agriculture and life cycle

  • The curing and carbon sink effect of hemp lime construction

  • How to reduce waste in the construction process and recycle excess materials
