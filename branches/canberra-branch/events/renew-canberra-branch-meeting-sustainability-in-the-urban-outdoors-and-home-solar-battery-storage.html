Renew Canberra Branch meeting - Sustainability in the urban outdoors and home solar/battery storage.

  • 2018-09-26 09:30:00
  • Corner of North Road and Geology Close, Acton ACT 2601

When: Wednesday 26 September 2018, 7:30 pm

Where: Ian Ross building #31, Australian National University, Corner of North Road and Geology Close, Acton ACT 2601. Click here for a campus map (Building #31 at G4 on North Rd) and here for a Google map. 

Presentations: 

  • Fraser Argue: Wicking beds for easier backyard gardening
  • Peter Abbott: The importance of Native Bees
  • Jeff Knowles: Batteries with your solar PV

This month we are going to start by hearing about sustainability in the garden. Fraser will show how wicking beds can be readily made in all shapes and sizes. They can be raised to make it harder for pests and weeds to invade and make gardening easier on your back.

Wicking beds are very productive, save water, and they stretch the intervals between watering. Fraser hopes to have others with wicking bed experience at the meeting to help answer any challenging questions.

Our native flora as well as food plants require pollination to survive - Peter will introduce you to our native bees and their role in sustaining our flora and assisting with crop pollination. Many solitary native bees live in our region – we need to encourage them to take up residence in our gardens.

Finally we step back inside with Jeff who has been evaluating a home battery storage system at his place. Anyone considering installing a battery storage system will benefit from what he has learned.

Fraser Argue is retired electrical engineer with a passionate interest in sustainability. He is a committee member of the local branch of Renew.

Peter Abbott is an experienced apiarist - honeybees first, now stingless social native bees. He is now focussing on the many species of solitary native bees.

Jeff Knowles is a teacher of philosophy but also an early adopter of PV and battery technology. He has been convenor of the Canberra Branch of Renew and is still an active member of the local committee.

This is a public meeting; please invite anyone who might be interested.

We hope to see you at this really interesting evening.
