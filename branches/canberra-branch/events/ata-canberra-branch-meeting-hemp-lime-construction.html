ATA Canberra branch meeting - Hemp lime construction

  • 2015-11-25 08:30:00
  • 31 North Road Acton ACT 2601

When: Wednesday 25 November, 2015, 7:30 pm
Where: Ian Ross Building, Australian National University, 31 North Road, Acton ACT 2601

Subject: Hemp lime construction. Carbon budget, energy efficiency, thermal performance and life cycle analysis.

Klara Marosszeky is the Managing Director of the Australian Hemp Masonry Company. Hemp lime construction is increasingly attracting global attention because it addresses several of our major challenges - carbon management, energy efficiency, thermally-effective healthy housing and the need to operate within a circular economy. Hemp lime building materials offer a sustainable solution to demands for zero-carbon construction and have major potential in restoration, retrofitting and new construction in the residential and commercial building sectors.

Wide adoption of Hemp lime construction would not only enable us to lock substantial amounts of carbon into infrastructure, it would also enable Australians to benefit from a broad range of manufacturing opportunities based on farming industrial hemp\'s high volume biomass.
