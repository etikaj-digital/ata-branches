Toowoomba Electric Vehicle Expo

  • 2016-10-16 00:30:00
  • 27 Lindsay St, Toowoomba, QLD

When: Sunday 16 October 2016, 10:30 am - 3:30 pm

Where: Cobb + Co Museum, 27 Lindsay St, Toowoomba, QLD. Click here for a map and can find details of how to get there here.

Cost: Free entry to Toowoomba Region residents.

View the main Toowoomba EV Expo page.

All people interested in electric vehicles and the future of motoring in Toowoomba are invited to come along to the Toowoomba Electric Vehicle (EV) Expo.

The EV Expo organised by the Toowoomba branch of the Alternative Technology Association (ATA) will showcase electric vehicles such as the Tesla S, BMW i3 and Nissan Leaf as well as electric bicycles. The vehicle owners will be available to discuss issues and answer questions for the public about the cost, performance and the many unique features of these vehicles.

There will also be a series of formal presentations by vehicle owners about purchasing and running electric vehicles including the intricacies of charging EVs.

One presentation will look at the controversial topic of driverless vehicles. It will be given by Associate Professor Dr Kieran Tranter of Griffith University. Driverless vehicles are closer to becoming commonplace on our roads than you may think!

The vehicles will be displayed in the forecourt of the Cobb+Co Museum on Lindsay St and the lectures will take place in the Museum theatrette. All the Museum exhibitions and facilities will be available to the public on the day. Admission to the museum is free to Toowoomba Region residents.

Seminar Program: Cobb+Co Museum Theatrette

11am Basics of owning an EV and off grid solar charging,
Gary Creighton, Australian Electric Vehicle Association, Brisbane
12noon Where to Next: Driverless Vehicles
Dr Kieran Tranter, Associate Professor, Griffith University
1pm How do electric vehicles stack up?
John Murray, Australian Electric Vehicle Association, Brisbane
2pm Charging Evs - how it works, different types
Les Smith, Australian Electric Vehicle Association, Brisbane

The event takes place with the support of the Toowoomba Regional Council.

We look forward to seeing you there,
